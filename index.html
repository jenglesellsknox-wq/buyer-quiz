Section 1 of 4
Financial Foundation
Have you checked your credit score in the last 90 days?
Why it matters: Your score determines if you qualify and at what rate. Higher score = lower rate = thousands saved.
  • 760+ — Best rates available
  • 700–759 — Competitive rates
  • 640–699 — Higher rates likely
  • Below 640 — Most lenders require 620+
Check free at AnnualCreditReport.com or your bank app.
How much do you have saved for a down payment?
  • 20%+ — Ideal. Avoids PMI (~$100–200/mo)
  • 10–19% — Solid. PMI applies.
  • 3.5% — FHA minimum
  • 3% — Some first-time buyer loans
On a $300k home: 20% = $60k / 3.5% = $10,500.
💡 Cash-limited? USDA and VA loans offer 100% financing — no down payment required. Ask Jon if you may qualify.
Do you have 2–6 months of living expenses saved as an emergency fund, separate from your down payment?
As a homeowner you cover repairs yourself. A single HVAC failure ($3,000–8,000) without a buffer can send you into debt right after closing. Keep this completely separate from your down payment.

Mortgage Readiness
Have you been pre-approved (not just pre-qualified) for a mortgage?
  • Pre-qualification — rough estimate, self-reported. Means little to sellers.
  • Pre-approval — hard credit pull + income docs. Sellers take this seriously.
In competitive markets many sellers won't show without a pre-approval letter.
What is your current debt-to-income (DTI) ratio situation?
DTI = monthly debts ÷ gross monthly income.
  • Under 36% — Ideal
  • 36–43% — Acceptable for most loans
  • 43–50% — Harder to qualify
  • 50%+ — Very difficult
Example: $5,000 income / $1,500 debts = 30% DTI ✅
How stable is your employment and income?
Lenders want 2 years of consistent employment in the same field.
  • W-2 — Easiest to qualify
  • Self-employed — 2 years of tax returns required
  • New job — Fine if same field or step up
  • Gaps — May need a letter of explanation
Avoid changing jobs right before applying.

Section 3 of 4
Home Search & Preferences
Have you defined your must-haves vs. nice-to-haves in a home?
Without clear priorities buyers waste time and make regrettable compromises. Define upfront:
  • Min bedrooms / bathrooms
  • School district (affects resale even without kids)
  • Max commute time
  • Yard, garage, HOA tolerance
  • Move-in ready vs. fixer-upper
Have you researched the neighborhoods or areas where you want to buy?
Location is the one thing you can't change. Research before touring:
  • Drive through at different times of day
  • School ratings at GreatSchools.org
  • Local crime statistics
  • FEMA flood zones — insurance adds $1,000–4,000/yr
  • Price trends by zip code

Section 4 of 4
Process & Team Readiness
Do you have a buyer's agent representing you?
The listing agent represents the seller. A buyer's agent represents you. They will:
  • Search MLS including off-market listings
  • Craft competitive offers and negotiate
  • Guide you through inspection, appraisal, closing
  • Typically cost you nothing — seller pays the commission
Do you understand the full costs of buying beyond the down payment?
Budget for these on top of your down payment:
  • Closing costs: 2–5% of loan amount
  • Inspection: $300–600
  • Appraisal: $400–700
  • Moving: $1,000–5,000+
  • Prepaids: Insurance, taxes, interest
On a $300k purchase, closing costs alone = $6,000–15,000.
What is your target timeline for closing on a home?
Once financially ready:
  • Pre-approval: 1–5 business days
  • House hunting: 2 weeks to 6+ months
  • Offer to closing: 30–60 days
Under 3 months? You need to be fully ready right now.
